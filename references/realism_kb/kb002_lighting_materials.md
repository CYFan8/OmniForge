# RAG-KB-002: Lighting & Materials for Image Generation

## Three-Point Lighting
- Key light: main source, 45 degrees from camera, defines form
- Fill light: opposite key, softens shadows, 50 percent key intensity
- Rim/back light: behind subject, separates from background, creates edge glow
- SD prompt: "three point lighting, rim lighting, professional studio lighting"

## Natural Light
- Golden hour: warm orange, long shadows, soft contrast, color temp 3500K
- Blue hour: cool blue, no direct sun, even exposure, color temp 9000K
- Overcast: soft diffused, minimal shadows, neutral color, even illumination
- Midday: harsh shadows, high contrast, color temp 5500K, unflattering
- SD prompt: "golden hour lighting, warm sunset, dramatic shadows"

## Material Properties
- Metal: specular highlights, sharp reflections, high contrast, environment map
- Glass: refraction, transparency, caustics, rim highlights, double reflection
- Fabric (silk): smooth specular, flowing folds, subtle sheen
- Fabric (cotton): diffuse, matte, soft folds, no sheen
- Fabric (denim): heavy weight, structured folds, slight texture
- Wood: grain pattern, diffuse reflection, warm tones, age patina
- Stone: rough texture, porous, irregular surface, subtle color variation
- SD prompt: "[material] texture, detailed [material] surface, realistic [material]"

## Shadow Principles
- Hard shadows: small/point light source, sharp edges, high contrast
- Soft shadows: large/diffuse source, gradual edges, low contrast
- Contact shadows: darkest where object touches surface, occlusion
- Ambient occlusion: darkening in crevices, corners, contact points
- SD prompt: "soft shadows, ambient occlusion, realistic shadow detail"

## Color Temperature
- Warm light (3000K): candle, sunrise, tungsten - orange/red cast
- Neutral (5500K): midday sun, flash - white balanced
- Cool light (7000K): overcast, shade, moonlight - blue cast
- Mix: warm key + cool fill = cinematic color contrast
- SD prompt: "warm lighting, cool shadows, cinematic color grading"
